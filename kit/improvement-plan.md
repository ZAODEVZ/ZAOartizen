# Improvement plan for the two Artizen projects

Fresh research pass (2026-07-01) on top of the existing playbook. Built from three parallel research agents
(live-platform scan, BCZ patronage conversion, ZAO Festivals fund-fit). Only NEW, actionable lifts are here.
The mechanics and the 48-hour playbook already live in `research/artizen-share-and-accept-playbook.md`.

  "ZAOstock Oct 3 text-overlay" artifact idea is rejected.

## Both projects (do these once, they lift everything)

1. **Enter multiple funds, one custom line each.** Single biggest multiplier. Each fund is a separate vote pool
   and match pool. About one hour per fund: read eligibility, write a 2-sentence "For [X]" qualifier. This is
   already the #1 move in the playbook and both fresh scans re-confirm it.
2. **Mobilize manual voters, not just buyers.** Curation is top ~30 percent BY VOTES, within each fund, and
   every vote is a manual click. Time-on-clicking is a curation signal. A dozen friends who each click through
   their votes beat one big buyer. Curation (votes) unlocks match; sales alone do not.
3. **Put social proof at the TOP of the page.** 3 to 5 short testimonials, above or right next to the Back
   button, not buried at the bottom. Top-of-page social proof is the single largest conversion lever in the
   research (cited 270 percent plus lift). We have WaveWarZ crew, Moses, Gneric, KOSBAAR to pull from.
4. **Post updates 2 to 3 times a week during the drive.** Silence kills momentum. Queue a Day-1 update NOW
   (thank early backers, one personal moment, one ask to share). Then mid-drive and final-48h updates.
5. **Add a 1-page pitch deck / HackMD link.** Curators and fund directors actually read it. No deck reads as
   low-effort. We already have `kit/bcz-deck.md` to adapt.
6. **Short square video Artifact beats a static image.** Under ~45 seconds, behind-the-scenes / process /
   event energy, no text. Video pulls more engagement on the leaderboard. Can be a clip, not a production.

## BetterCallZaal Strategies (patronage - keep the honest framing)

The framing is locked and correct: a fund to help artists full-time, back it if you would value Zaal in your
corner. The risk is drifting into a paid-services menu, which kills patronage. Improvements that respect that:

1. **The title reads like consulting.** "Strategies" sounds like a services firm. Warmer patron-first options
   to consider (Zaal picks, or keeps current): "In Your Corner," "Artists Full-Time," "Back Me, I Back You."
   Low effort, worth a look. Framing stays the same either way.
2. **Open with the personal WHY, not a tool list.** 2 to 3 sentences: who Zaal is, why helping artists
   full-time matters to him, what he has already built, why he needs backers to do it full-time. Emotion drives
   patronage; feature lists do not.
3. **Describe the one reward as a RELATIONSHIP, not a deliverable.** Keep the single "In Your Corner" reward.
   Word it: "You back my work. I am in yours - including putting my 80-plus open-source tools and agent stack
   to work for what you are building." Do NOT itemize ("free code review, 1:1 call, design feedback") - an
   itemized menu makes it transactional and patrons leave.
4. **Frontload the proof numbers.** Near the top: 80-plus open-source repos, WaveWarZ 459 SOL volume / 950
   battles / 7.8 SOL to artists, The ZAO 0 to 250-plus artists in 3 years. Numbers are the "he ships" proof.
5. **1 to 2 testimonials above the Back button** (see shared move 3). For BCZ specifically: a line from a
   WaveWarZ co-founder or a ZAO builder Zaal helped ship something.
6. **Warm the backer list before pushing.** Patronage funds with no pre-warmed audience stall. Ping the ZAO
   core, WaveWarZ crew, and Farcaster friends directly before the broad push.
7. **Optional, low priority:** a higher "Steward" perk unlocked by buying several Artifacts (a decoy anchor can
   lift average contribution). Only if it does not clutter the clean single-reward. Test, do not lead with it.

## ZAO Festivals

Strong bones already (real event history, WaveWarZ proof, the Oct 3 Ellsworth flagship). Lifts:

1. **Lead with PLACE and community, then The ZAO.** Open: "Ellsworth, Maine, the gateway to Acadia National
   Park - a free outdoor music festival." Place-based / local-impact framing raises more than network framing.
   The Acadia "4 million visitors a year" and the Art of Ellsworth / Maine Craft Weekend tie-in are the hook.
2. **Proof narrative = proven organizers.** "12 artists in NYC 2024 (ZAO-PALOOZA), 10 musicians at Miami Art
   Basel 2024 (ZAO-CHELLA), WaveWarZ 950 battles." Add Steve Peer's 430 house concerts since 1989 as the deep
   local-Maine tradition. This de-risks backers.
3. **Recurring-series framing, not one-off.** "Year 1 of the annual ZAO Festivals flagship." Recurring framing
   drives repeat donation and sponsor interest far more than a single event.
4. **Transparent budget line-items.** "Artist fees, permits, staging, promotion, contingency" with rough
   percentages. Line-item trust raises average donation materially.
5. **Backer lineup vote.** "Backers vote which emerging artists get a slot." Engagement plus FOMO; voters then
   market the festival to their own networks.
6. **Stretch goals unlock artists.** Base goal hit, then each additional chunk adds an emerging-artist slot.
   Keeps momentum alive after 100 percent and re-engages during slow days.
7. **Rewards as perks on $10 buys** (not priced passes): buy 1 = name on the backer wall; buy a handful = merch
   / artist shoutout; buy a bigger stack = lineup vote or a festival credit. Map perks to cumulative Artifacts.
8. **Artifact:** keep the WaveWarZ-at-ZAO-CHELLA square (real proof, integral-art logo is fine). A second
   Artifact of raw festival energy or the Ellsworth coastline is good IF it carries no text.
9. **Fund fit (VERIFY on the live S6 site first).** Candidate categories: music / culture and community arts /
   film and visual arts (for the ZAO-CHELLA reels) / public goods / place-based-local / regenerative-gatherings
   (Greenpill). One "For [X]" line each:
   - Culture and community arts: "For place-based, artist-led culture in rural Maine."
   - Music / global music: "For free, live, original music that pays emerging artists."
   - Film / visual arts: "For the documentary and reels of a real DIY festival."
   - Public goods / regenerative gatherings: "For a free community gathering that strengthens a local economy."
