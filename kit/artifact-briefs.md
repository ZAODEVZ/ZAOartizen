# Artizen Artifact briefs - ZAO Festivals + BCZ Strategies

Generated by a 106-agent workflow (52 concepts/project judged on a 50-pt rubric: scroll-stop, essence, square-no-text, motion, brand-fit). Top 3 buildable briefs per project. Artifact rules: square 1:1, ZERO text, GIF/short video best, $10 open edition.


## ZAO Festivals

### 1. Crowd Light Surge

**Concept:** Dense crowd silhouettes in pure navy fill the frame, hands raised toward a performer backlit at the stage base. A sharp gold light wash pulses upward from the stage every half-second, flooding across the raised hands and crowd profiles in hard relief—no gradients, pure contrast. The light surge creates a strobe-like rhythm that matches musical energy, making the crowd sway and arms rise higher with each pulse.

**Shot list:** 1. Source a crowd shot from past ZAO event (NYC, Miami, WaveWarZ, or COC Concertz) showing dense audience with 20+ raised hands under stage lighting, shot from stage-POV or low angle during high-energy moment. 2. Extract performer silhouette or find a backlit stage-center artist (1-2 frames from crowd footage or separate stage shot). 3. Pull stage-light footage or rig video showing gold/amber spotlights sweeping or pulsing (can be separate B-roll, any festival with stage rigs). 4. In AE or CapCut: silhouette the crowd (desaturate to pure black, crush shadows) and place on navy #0a1628 background. 5. Layer the gold light sweep as a top layer using Screen or Lighten blend mode. 6. Keyframe the light layer opacity to pulse every 0.5s (0% → 100% → 0%) over 2-3s total, looping seamlessly. 7. Add subtle crowd sway motion (vertical scale/position keyframes on crowd layer, ±3% height variation, tied to light pulse). 8. Grade entire comp to crush midtones to navy, push highlights to saturated gold #f5a623. Render as 1:1 square, 3-second loop.

**Source footage:** NYC ZAO-PALOOZA or NFT NYC footage (crowd with hands raised), Miami ZAO-CHELLA WaveWarZ LIVE moment, or COC Concertz outdoor stage with audience. Prioritize clips shot during peak energy with visible stage lighting and multiple raised hands. Secondary: any festival rig footage showing gold/amber spotlights or strobes firing—can be sourced separately if needed.

**Style:** Pure navy shadow (#0a1628) as base, zero mid-tone detail. Gold (#f5a623) arrives sharp and saturated—no gradients, hard edges on light sweep. Pace: steady 0.5-second pulse repeating 4-5x per 3-second loop, mechanical and hypnotic, not random. Mood: collective euphoria, sacred stage moment, timeless. High contrast = high energy = buyer wants in.

**Tools:** Adobe After Effects for keyframe animation and blend-mode light sweep, OR CapCut for faster turnaround (light effects + motion keyframes). Export 3-second 1:1 square MP4 or WebM, 30fps, H.264. If sourcing separate rig footage, composite in AE using Lighten blend for light layer. Desaturation/crush blacks via Curves adjustment layer.

**Why it wins:** Highest-scoring concept (46/50). Pure spectacle—high-contrast black-and-gold is Instagram-native and stops scroll instantly. The pulsing light creates hypnotic rhythm that reads as 'live music moment' in under 1 second. Silhouette composition is universal (no faces to age, language, or alienate). Gold-on-navy is The ZAO brand signature. Buyers see themselves in the crowd: 'this could be me at ZAOstock.' Looping pulse mimics a heartbeat—buyers will replay it.

### 2. Hands to the Light

**Concept:** First-person immersion from inside the crowd looking toward the stage. Silhouetted hands and raised arms frame the foreground in deep shadow; a performer backlit by a sharp gold spotlight cuts through the frame center, mid-gesture (arm raised or mid-jump). Horizontal light rays sweep across the scene every 2 seconds. The perspective fractionally tilts upward as the moment peaks, making viewers feel they're lifting their own gaze toward the stage.

**Shot list:** 1. Source crowd/audience POV footage from ground level during peak performance (ZAO-PALOOZA NYC, ZAO-CHELLA Miami, COC Concertz, or WaveWarZ LIVE)—ideally 60fps for motion smoothing. 2. Extract foreground silhouettes of raised hands/arms from the chosen clip (or shoot 30 seconds of hands raised if clarity is needed). 3. Locate or source a performer on stage backlit by gold/warm spotlights, shot from audience angle—separate clip OK if crowd POV footage lacks clear performer. 4. Pull stage-light sweep footage (horizontal light beams cutting through haze or crowd). 5. In AE or Premiere: layer the silhouetted crowd hands in the foreground (desaturated, nearly black). 6. Position performer silhouette in center-to-background with gold backlight glow (use Outer Glow or Screen layer). 7. Animate light-sweep overlays to travel left-to-right and back every 2.5 seconds, using Scale and Position keyframes. 8. Keyframe subtle camera tilt (+1 to +2 degrees) during the light's rightward sweep (peak energy), reset at sweep end. 9. Add subtle hand-raise motion (foreground layer rises ~5-10px at peak light moment, falls back). 10. Grade: crush blacks to navy, saturate gold in highlights. Render 3-second loop, 1:1 square.

**Source footage:** 1. Audience-level crowd footage from any ZAO festival showing raised hands/arms backlit or side-lit (ideally during applause or peak energy). 2. Performer on stage, gold-lit from behind or side, shot from audience angle (can be different clip). 3. Stage-light or rig footage showing horizontal sweep or cutting beams (overhead or from audience view). 4. If primary source lacks clarity, shoot a 2-minute reference video of the same POV angle (hands, stage, lights) to extract clean layers.

**Style:** Navy (#0a1628) shadows dominate the lower 60% (crowd/hands). Upper 40% is gold-washed (stage, performer, light). Gradual transition between zones, not hard edge. Pace: slow and intentional—2.5-second light sweep feels musical, not frantic. Mood: intimate congregation, spiritual gathering, safe ascension. Subtlety matters here; overt glitch or flicker would ruin immersion.

**Tools:** Premiere Pro or After Effects for camera motion and layered light sweeps. Use Outer Glow, Screen blend mode, and subtle zoom/pan for the tilt effect. CapCut if speed is priority (though camera tilt is easier in AE). Export 1:1 square 30fps MP4. Slow-mo source footage (60fps) benefits from framerate conversion to 30fps for motion smoothing.

**Why it wins:** Second-strongest execution (44/50) and offers immersion that the silhouette-from-above shot does not. First-person POV is psychologically powerful—'I am there, I am part of this.' The subtle camera tilt (looking up) triggers embodied response; buyers physically mimic the motion. Silhouetted hands in foreground create visual depth and framing that holds attention. Gold sweep across the frame has cinematic quality. Uniquely different from #1 (Crowd Light Surge) while maintaining same navy/gold palette. Buyers feel agency and presence, not just observation.

### 3. Euphoria Loop

**Concept:** A tight 1:1 shot of 8-10 hands raised in silhouette against luminous gold stage light, with navy-black background below. Hands are sculptural, backlit and rim-lit in sharp outline—some nearly vertical, others tilted at varied angles, all reaching upward in unified gesture. The hands move in synchronized vertical rhythm, rising upward over 1.5 seconds, reaching peak height, then falling back down. As they move, the gold stage light pulses in sync, brightest at peak reach, creating a breathing, heartbeat-like effect.

**Shot list:** 1. Source close-up slow-motion crowd footage showing 8-15 raised hands under warm stage lighting (any ZAO festival: NYC, Miami, WaveWarZ LIVE, COC Concertz). Prioritize 60fps or higher source for motion smoothing. Frame should be tight on hands only—no faces, no crowd bodies, just the gesture. 2. Extract the hands clip as a 2-3 second sequence at peak energy. 3. Source stage-light rig footage showing gold/amber strobes pulsing (separate B-roll OK). 4. In AE: desaturate the hands to pure silhouette (crush blacks to near-black, crush highlights to white/light gray for rim light). 5. Layer on navy #0a1628 background. 6. Keyframe vertical position and scale of hands layer: rise from center position +20% height over 1.5s, hold peak for 0.3s, fall back down over 1.5s, reset. 7. Keyframe gold light-overlay opacity: 0% at frame start, ramp to 100% as hands reach peak, hold, then ramp back to 0% as hands fall. 8. Add quick 100-200ms gold strobes every 0.4 seconds throughout (simulate stage lighting rig). 9. Ensure loop reset is seamless (final frame = opening frame). Render 3-second loop, 1:1 square, 30fps.

**Source footage:** Tight close-up or medium-wide shot of crowd hands raised during peak ZAO festival moment (NYC, Miami, WaveWarZ LIVE, or COC Concertz). 60fps slow-motion source is ideal for the vertical motion to read smooth. If close-ups are not available, composite 8-10 hand silhouettes extracted from wider footage and arrange in a natural raised formation. Stage-rig B-roll (separate pulsing lights) adds authenticity.

**Style:** Navy (#0a1628) solid background, zero texture. Hands are pure silhouette—near-black with sharp white rim light where stage light catches them. Gold (#f5a623) light arrives as gradient fill or glow, never harsh. Pace: 3-second loop with 1.5-second rise and fall creates dreamy, non-frantic energy. Quick strobes (100-200ms every 0.4s) add live-show texture without overwhelming. Mood: sacred, euphoric, meditative. This is the 'why we go to festivals' moment.

**Tools:** After Effects for precise keyframe animation and strobe timing. Use Position/Scale keyframes for hand motion, Opacity keyframes for light pulse. Quick strobes achieved via Adjustment Layer with keyframed Opacity or using an animated gold gradient layer. CapCut can do the hand motion and light pulse, but strobes require manual frame-level work (easier in AE). Export 1:1 square 30fps MP4.

**Why it wins:** Pure emotion in a single gesture. The unified upward motion is universally read as 'collective joy' or 'worship' in seconds. Hands are the most recognizable human element—buyers see their own hands reaching. The breathing pulse (hands up / light bright, hands down / light dim) creates a meditative, trance-like quality that makes viewers loop it. Third-strongest concept (44/50) and differentiates from #1 (crowd-wide spectacle) by zooming into the intimate hand-reach. Gold strobes add urgency and 'liveness.' Euphoria is the highest-intent emotion for festival attendance; this animates that directly.


## BetterCallZaal Strategies

### 1. The Moment (Hands in Flow)

**Concept:** POV from Zaal's seated position at desk: both hands settling onto a laptop keyboard, then typing in fast, deliberate rhythm. High-contrast lighting with warm desk lamp and cool screen glow illuminating skin and keys. The frame captures the exact second of transition from stillness to flow - the breath before work begins. 2-3 second seamless loop showing the intensity and focus of a builder in deep work.

**Shot list:** 1. Overhead close-up video of Zaal's hands at desk (eye-level POV looking down at his own lap/keyboard). Shoot 10-15s minimum of raw typing footage, hand resting on keys, fingers in motion, at least one hand-to-mouse transition. 2. Desk lamp positioned to one side, creating directional rim-light on hands/keyboard. 3. Laptop and second monitor visible but soft-focus in background glow. 4. Capture real work time (morning or evening), no posed/choreographed motion. 5. Additional takes: hands lifting off, resettling, slight pause in typing (the breath). Aspect: 1:1 square framing. Angle: shoot from 45-degree overhead or directly above.

**Source footage:** Raw footage: Zaal at his desk during actual work session (Telegram, code editor, Farcaster, agent dashboards visible on screen but out-of-focus is fine). Desk setup: current workspace with laptop, secondary monitor if available, desk lamp (tungsten preferred for warm color), papers/coffee mug (natural desk clutter). Lighting: single directional light (the desk lamp itself), no flat overhead lights. Minimal post-processing - the loop lives in the framing and rhythm, not effects.

**Style:** High contrast, warm tungsten overhead desk lamp + cool monitor glow. Navy background with gold rim-light on hands. Cinematic, intimate, documentary feel. Dark shadows except where light rakes across the desk surface. No color grading - natural warm/cool contrast is the aesthetic.

**Tools:** CapCut or Final Cut Pro (preferred): Import 10-15s overhead video clip, crop to 1:1 square center-frame to isolate hands/keyboard, color-correct to boost warm (tungsten) tones and cool (screen) tones, add subtle vignette (darken edges ~15% to frame the hands as focal point). Trim and loop-point the clip to find the cleanest 2-3s cycle (typing → pause → return). Optional: add 1-frame micro-stutters on key press for hyper-detail if shot at 60fps. Export as MP4 (H.264, 1080x1080, 24fps, 5-8 Mbps) or GIF (cinemagraph style, 800x800px). No text overlays.

**Why it wins:** It's the most iconic image of entrepreneurship - hands moving, keyboard lit, focused work. No metaphor, no abstraction, just DOING. Stops the scroll because it's hypnotic (hands in motion hook the eye), intimate (POV makes the viewer Zaal), and relatable (every builder sees themselves here). The high-contrast lighting is cinematic and immediately recognizable. It's buildable from footage Zaal already has on his Mac. The loop is silent but the rhythm of typing implies sound - a full sensory experience. Short, sharp, powerful.

### 2. Builders Rally (Network Activation)

**Concept:** Top-down aerial view of Zaal's central workspace (desk, hands typing, screens glowing, coffee mug, scattered papers) in navy + gold tones. As the camera slowly pulls back and rises, multiple other builder desks/workspaces spiral into the frame around the center in a radiating pattern - each lighting up on arrival (screen turn-on effect, hands settling, subtle glow). Creates a node-and-network feeling: Zaal at the hub, other builders activating around him. The visual reads as a community of makers rallying together, all in flow at the same moment.

**Shot list:** 1. Close-up overhead (90-degree downward angle) of Zaal's workspace desk while he works - capture hands in motion, screen glow, papers shifting, coffee cup, lamp. Shoot 4-5s of active typing/working. 2. Secondary footage of 3-4 other builder desks (friends, collaborators, BCZ circle members, coworking community) each shot overhead at same angle and in same lighting conditions if possible. Each should show: active hands (typing, gesturing), at least one screen with glow, desk objects (coffee, notebook, phone). Shoot 2-3s per additional desk. 3. Lighting control: tungsten desk lamp on each workspace, minimal overhead light, let screen glow be the secondary light source. 4. No faces required - the focus is hands and workspaces. 5. All clips in 1:1 square framing, shot at same resolution/format for consistency.

**Source footage:** Primary: existing overhead footage or photo of Zaal's workspace from recent /worksession or coworking session. If video doesn't exist, shoot a fresh 5s clip next time he's at his desk. Secondary: ask 3-4 people in BCZ circle or recent coworking attendees (Iman, Cannon, Samantha, etc.) to send 2-3s overhead video/screen-record of their own desks while working. Frame should be 1:1 square, desk should be lit similarly (desk lamp + screen), and they should show active hands/work-in-motion. Audio: mute all or keep ambient desk sounds (typing, subtle keyboard clicks) layered under the visuals, no dialogue. Color palette: navy (#0a1628) + gold (#f5a623) grading applied uniformly to all clips in post.

**Style:** Top-down/bird's-eye perspective. Navy backgrounds with gold accents catching light. Desks are real workspace clutter - screens, keyboards, hands, coffee cups, notebooks - not sterile. Warm tungsten and cool monitor glow create depth. Smooth, seamless camera movement (no cuts). Feeling: energetic but not chaotic, coordinated, purposeful momentum. Color grading: slight desaturation for focus, gold highlights punch. 24fps for smooth loop.

**Tools:** After Effects or Premiere Pro: (1) Import all 5-8 desk clips and scale/position in a Premiere sequence. (2) Start with Zaal's desk centered at full frame (2-3s). (3) Keyframe camera position (simulated via scale + position keyframes) to pull back/up over 1.5-2s, revealing first secondary desk spiraling in from left at ~45 degrees (slight rotation). (4) As zoom continues, 2-3 more desks light up around the perimeter (staggered keyframes, each desk enters 0.3-0.5s apart). At furthest zoom (showing all 5 desks in a ring around Zaal's center), hold for 0.5s. (5) Keyframe a 'screen turn on' effect for each arriving desk: brightness ramp from 30% to 100% over 0.4s as it enters frame. (6) Add subtle glow/bloom around each desk lamp and screen edge (+20% saturation on gold accents). (7) Loop: keyframe zoom + position back to center/close-up (1.5s reverse motion) and hard-cut back to start OR dissolve-fade and hard-cut. Total loop: 2.8-3.5s. Export as MP4 1080x1080, 24fps. Optional: add a very faint ambient sound bed (desk ambience, subtle typing underneath, 2-3 dB normalized).

**Why it wins:** It's unique and memorable. The overhead-to-zoom-out motion is inherently interesting and holds attention. But the killer insight is what it REPRESENTS: BCZ is not about Zaal as a solo builder - it's about Zaal at the center of a network of builders, rallying them, helping them ship. Every builder backing the collectible sees themselves as part of this ring. The visual language is web3-native (nodes, network, activation) without being cheesy. It's aspirational (who doesn't want to be part of an activated community?) and true to BCZ's actual model. Hard to copy. Buildable with real community footage. The loop is smooth and hypnotic - the endless activation cycle mirrors the energy of a startup in hypergrowth.

### 3. The Leap (Bet on Yourself)

**Concept:** Silhouette of a figure in mid-jump, shot from below looking upward. The body is arced mid-vault - committed, suspended between ground and light above. Backlit with a warm golden flare at the apex. Background transitions from deep navy shadow at bottom (ground) to bright gold/white at top (sky/opportunity). Entirely silhouetted - no facial features, just the profile of will and momentum in one gesture. The image is the visual metaphor for 'I'm betting on myself' - the moment of leaving solid ground.

**Shot list:** 1. Zaal filmed outdoors in golden hour (sunrise or sunset, 20 min before/after peak) OR in a studio with backlighting setup. Low-angle upward camera position (shoot from knee-height looking up). 2. Zaal performs a clean vertical jump or vault leap - explosive, athletic, decisive (not a small hop). Shoot in 60fps at 4K if possible, to allow smooth slow-motion playback at 2-3s loop duration. Capture 2-3 takes for best form. 3. Backlight: single key light behind subject (stage light, large reflector with sunlight bouncing, or studio backlight gel). Subject should be 70-80% silhouetted against this backlight. 4. Foreground: minimal - just grass, concrete, or studio floor fading to shadow. 5. Framing: 1:1 square, subject centered at frame, tight headroom (the silhouette peaks at frame top-third).

**Source footage:** Option A (Outdoor): Film Zaal jumping during golden hour in a location with clean background (empty field, park, beach, or simple backdrop). Ideal: clear sky or gradient cloud cover as the background (which becomes the gold-to-white gradient in post). Option B (Studio): Zaal in a dark studio space with a strong backlight (spotlight or bounce) positioned behind him. Subject is front-lit minimally to keep silhouette. Required: 60fps source video, 4K if possible. Clothing: solid-color top (dark or neutral) to read as clean silhouette. Audio: none needed (motion is the message). Aspect: frame to 1:1 square in shooting or in post.

**Style:** Backlighting with silhouette (zero detail on body, all light and shadow). Golden-hour or studio key light positioned behind and slightly above subject. Deep navy gradient at frame bottom fading to bright gold/white at top. Cinematography: low-angle upward camera, athletic form. No color grading needed - the gradient and backlighting ARE the aesthetic. Feels heroic, aspirational, real, not CGI or gimmicky.

**Tools:** Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve: (1) Import 60fps source video of jump sequence. (2) In the timeline, slow down to ~40% (or 60% depending on jump arc duration) to stretch the 1.5-2s jump into a 3-4s loop. (3) Crop/scale to 1:1 square aspect ratio, center-frame on silhouette. (4) Color grade: (a) desaturate all color (B&W silhouette extraction via Lumetri or curves - black point at silhouette, white point at sky). (b) Overlay a navy-to-gold gradient adjustment layer: navy (#0a1628) at bottom 50% of frame, fading to gold (#f5a623) in mid-frame, fading to bright white (#f0f0f0) at top 30%. Use gradient map or adjustment layer with ~30% opacity blend to let the jump silhouette show through. (5) In Resolve color page: use the vector scope to isolate the backlighting halo and boost it slightly (+15% luminance on the rim). (6) Loop point: the jump should start at frame 0 (grounded, crouched), peak at ~60% through (airborne, backlighting flare maximum), land and finish at end, then hard-cut back to frame 0 for seamless repeat. (7) Export as MP4 1080x1080, 24fps, H.264.

**Why it wins:** It's THE image of entrepreneurship, risk, and 'betting on yourself' in one gesture. No ambiguity, no words needed. The silhouette makes it iconic and universally readable - anyone can project themselves into this shape. The gradient (navy-to-gold) is BCZ's brand palette, so it feels on-brand without being on-the-nose. The backlighting halo creates a sense of transcendence - the figure is moving toward the light, toward success, toward their goal. It's aspirational without being preachy. The loop is hypnotic (watching the jump repeat creates a sense of unstoppable momentum). It's a single, powerful image that encapsulates why someone would back Zaal: he's someone willing to leap, and he helps others leap too. Totally buildable with one afternoon of filming outdoors or in a studio. The metaphor is so clear that even without context, a viewer on Artizen will immediately 'get it.'
